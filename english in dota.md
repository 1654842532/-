#### Invoker

With an intricate([ˈɪn.trɪ.kət]<adj.>错综复杂的;难以理解的) arsenal([ˈɑː.sən.əl]<n.>武器库;军火库) of spells at his disposal([dɪˈspəʊ.zəl]<n.>清楚,处理), Invoker can adapt to any battle situation. Each configuration(布局,构造) of his three spell components yields of one of ten spells, meaning he's never without a way to destroy or escape his enemies.

##### Biography

In its earliest, and some would say most potent([ˈpəʊ.tənt]<adj.>强有力的,强大的) form, magic was primarily the art of memory. It required no technology, no wands or appurtenances([əˈpɜː.tən.əns]<n.>(反映某种生活方式的)所有物,标志性物品) other than the mind of the magician. All the trappings([ˈtræp.ɪŋz]<n.>(特定工作、处境获事件的)从属物,标志) of ritual([ˈrɪtʃ.u.əl]<n.>例行公事,老规矩;(尤指)仪式) were merely mnemonic([nɪˈmɒn.ɪk]<n.>帮助记忆的诗歌;助记顺口溜) devices, meant to allow the practitioners([prækˈtɪʃ.ən.ər]<n.>执业者;从业人员) to recall in rich detail the specific mental formulae that unlocked a spell's power.     

The greatest mages in those days were the ones blessed with the greatest memories, and yet so complex were the invocations([ˌɪn.vəˈkeɪ.ʃən]<n.>援引,引用) that all wizards were forced to specialize.
